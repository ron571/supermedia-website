# Link Building Strategy — supermedia.co.nz
*June 2026*

---

## Overview

Super Media operates in a niche with structural advantages for link building: genuinely independent advice in an industry full of conflicted players, 35 years of verifiable expertise, and a free tool (Superscan) that gives journalists and bloggers something to reference. The strategy below is sequenced from lowest effort to highest, with the highest-value opportunities identified clearly.

NZ's digital advertising market hit $2.967B in 2025 (up 12% YoY). SME ad spend is growing. The editorial interest in "is your agency ripping you off?" angles is perennial. That's the frame to work from.

---

## Tier 2 — Editorial Links (Medium effort, high value)

These require pitching content or expertise to NZ publications. Each successful placement delivers a high-DA link and direct referral traffic from an engaged audience.

### 2.1 StopPress (stoppress.co.nz)

**Why:** DA ~50+, the definitive hub for NZ advertising and comms industry. Readership is marketing managers, agency heads, brand managers — exactly Super Media's audience. 9,500+ email subscribers.

**How to get in:**  
StopPress accepts contributed content. Email: [email protected]

**Angle ideas that would work for StopPress:**
- *"What NZ advertisers still don't know about how agency rebates work"* — Ron's inside knowledge of the agency model is genuinely newsworthy here
- *"We analysed 50 NZ media plans. Here's what we found."* — data-led, shareable
- *"The AI media tools NZ agencies are actually using (vs. what they're selling)"* — timely, contrarian
- *"Five questions to ask your agency before renewing the contract"* — practical, high shareability

**Target:** 1 contributed article per quarter.

### 2.2 M+AD! — NZ Media and Advertising News Daily (mad-daily.com)

**Why:** Daily publication, highly indexed, read by media buyers, planners, and brand managers. Smaller but highly targeted.

**How to get in:** Submit news tips and opinion pieces. Ron's independence gives him a unique angle — most people who comment on the NZ media industry have a commercial stake in it. Ron can comment critically in a way others can't.

**Angle ideas:**
- Commentary on specific NZ media deals or channel shifts
- Response pieces to industry reports (IAB quarterly data, Nielsen spend reports)
- Short opinion on agency model changes

**Target:** 1 news mention or opinion piece per quarter.

### 2.3 NZ Marketing Magazine (nzmarketingmag.co.nz / scgmedia.co.nz)

**Why:** Print and digital quarterly, NZ's only dedicated marketing title. DA solid, read by CMOs and senior marketers — Super Media's ideal buyers.

**How to get in:** Contact SCG Media editorial team. Pitch a column or case study contribution. The "independent advisor" angle is rare in their contributor mix.

**Angle:** A regular column on media buying from the client's perspective would be genuinely differentiated — there's no one else writing this in NZ from a non-agency position.

**Target:** One placement or column pitch per half-year.

### 2.4 Stuff.co.nz / NZ Herald Business Section

**Why:** DA 80+, massive referral traffic, builds mass credibility.

**How:** These require a news hook, not just a pitch. Viable angles:

- *"NZ small businesses are wasting millions on digital ads — here's the data"* — use Superscan aggregated results if available
- When IAB or Nielsen release NZ ad spend data, Ron can be a quoted independent commentator. Email the journalist who covered the last report and introduce yourself as an available independent source.
- If a high-profile agency scandal or rebate story breaks in NZ, Ron is the only truly independent voice who can comment.

**Tactic:** Build journalist relationships now so when a story breaks you're the first call. Find 3–4 business/marketing journalists at Stuff and NZ Herald on LinkedIn and connect with a brief intro.

## Tier 3 — Thought Leadership & Speaking (Highest effort, highest long-term value)

These take longer to convert to links but build the kind of authority that compounds.

### 3.1 NZ Marketing Association Events

The NZMA runs regular events — Brainy Breakfasts, webinars, Monthly Marketing Meetups — and actively looks for speakers with real-world expertise. A session on *"How to tell if your agency is working for you or for the media owner"* would draw strong attendance.

**Outcome:** Event listing page with link to supermedia.co.nz, coverage in NZMA newsletter, potential StopPress or M+AD! mention.

**Action:** Join NZMA, then pitch to speak at a 2026 event. Contact: marketing.org.nz/about-events-conferences

### 3.2 Podcasts

NZ has an active business podcast ecosystem. A 30-minute interview on the right show reaches an engaged audience and typically results in a show notes page with a link.

Target podcasts:
- **The Icehouse Podcast** — NZ SME growth focus, large audience
- **NZ Business Podcast** — Broad SME audience
- **StopPress Podcasts** — Industry audience (stoppress-podcasts.stoppress.co.nz)
- **The Marketing Freaks** — NZ marketing podcast

**Pitch angle:** "An independent media advisor explains what agencies don't want you to know about your ad budget"

**Action:** Pitch to 5 podcasts in Q3 2026. Keep the pitch to 3 sentences: who Ron is, what the angle is, why their audience would care.

### 3.3 LinkedIn as a Link Multiplier

LinkedIn posts don't directly build backlinks, but they amplify the content that does. When Ron publishes on StopPress or gets quoted in NZ Herald, a LinkedIn post to his network drives shares, which drives further pickups.

**Tactic:** Publish one substantive LinkedIn article per month on NZ media buying topics. These index in Google and establish topical authority.

## Tier 4 — Linkable Assets on supermedia.co.nz

Build content that other sites want to link to without being asked.

### 4.1 NZ Media Rate Benchmarking Guide

A publicly available guide to what NZ businesses should expect to pay for TV, radio, digital display, programmatic, and social — benchmarked against real market rates. Nothing like this exists publicly in NZ.

**Why it earns links:** Journalists, marketing blogs, accountants, and business advisors will reference it. It's a genuinely useful resource that doesn't exist.

**Effort:** High (Ron needs to populate the numbers). Outcome: Evergreen links.

### 4.2 "Is Your Agency Ripping You Off?" Quiz/Tool

An expanded version of Superscan with a shareable result. Already partly there — lean into the shareability angle.

**Tactic:** When someone completes a Superscan, prompt them to share their result on LinkedIn. Each share is a mention of supermedia.co.nz.

### 4.3 NZ Advertising Spend Data Commentary

Each time IAB NZ or Nielsen publish quarterly spend data (which happens regularly), publish a short analysis piece on the Thinking section of the site within 48 hours of the release. These posts often pick up links from the publications covering the data.

**Why it works:** Journalists need independent commentary on data. If your site already has analysis up, they may link to it rather than write their own.

## What to Avoid

- **Link exchanges or paid links** — Google penalises these and they're not needed here
- **Generic "marketing agency" directories** that list thousands of global agencies — low relevance, low value
- **Spammy outreach templates** — Ron's credibility is the asset; personalised, specific pitches only
- **Over-investing in social media links** — nofollow, minimal SEO value; useful for traffic but not link authority

---

## Measuring Progress

Track monthly in a simple spreadsheet:
- Total referring domains (via Ahrefs free / Semrush / Moz)
- Domain Rating (DR) trend
- Google Search Console: impressions and clicks for target keywords
- Target keywords to rank: "independent media consultant NZ", "media audit NZ", "NZ media buying", "programmatic advertising NZ"

A realistic 12-month outcome from executing this strategy is 30–50 new referring domains, a meaningful DR increase, and first-page rankings for 3–5 target keywords.
